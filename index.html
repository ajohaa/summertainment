About the Badlands

Located in the Black Hills region of western South Dakota, the dramatic landscapes of Badlands National Park span layered rock formations, steep canyons and towering spires. Bison, bighorn sheep and prairie dogs inhabit its sprawling grasslands. The Badlands Loop Road (Highway 240) winds past scenic lookouts. Several trails begin near the Ben Reifel Visitor Center on the east side of the park, and is the main spot for information in the North Unit. The Fossil Exhibit Trail is a boardwalk with displays on fossils uncovered in the park.
